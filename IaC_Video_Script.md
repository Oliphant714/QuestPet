## [SLIDE 3 — What is Infrastructure as Code?]
**[1:00 – 1:45]**

In the old days, if the tech team needed to set up a new server for a marketing landing page, they had to log in and manually click through dozens of menus. It was slow and prone to human error.

With Infrastructure as Code, we throw out the manual checklist. Instead, we write a literal digital script — a blueprint — that automatically sets up our servers, databases, and networks.

Here's the Marketing translation: Think of IaC like an automated email marketing sequence. Instead of manually typing and sending an onboarding email to every new lead, you write the template once, set the rules, and the software deploys it instantly and flawlessly every time.

That's exactly what IaC does for our infrastructure.

---

## [SLIDE 4 — Modularity]
**[1:45 – 2:30]**

Now let's talk about Modularity.

In our system, a "module" is simply a collection of digital pieces that we manage together as a single unit. Think of it like a Lego set.

Instead of reinventing the wheel every time we need a new environment, our developers build reusable child modules and store them in a private registry — essentially a library of pre-approved digital building blocks.

Here's the brand team translation: Imagine you're launching a regional campaign. You don't design a brand-new logo, font, and disclaimer from scratch for every single market. You pull from your pre-approved Brand Asset Kit.

In IaC, our "root module" is the overall campaign workspace, and the "child modules" are those pre-approved kits — for logos, email templates, landing pages. This ensures every new digital space we build is identical, secure, and deploys in seconds.

---

## [SLIDE 5 — State Management]
**[2:30 – 3:10]**

Finally, we have State Management. Because our infrastructure is automated, our system needs a way to remember exactly what it has built. It does this by saving a State file.

The State file maps our digital blueprints to the actual, real-world servers currently running in the cloud. It keeps everything in sync.

Here's the Sales translation — and this one is really direct: Think of the State file exactly like Salesforce or your CRM.

If a sales rep doesn't log their client calls in the CRM, the team loses track of the account. You might accidentally call the same client twice, or pitch the wrong product.

Similarly, the State file is our technical CRM. It ensures our system knows exactly what assets are live right now — preventing us from duplicating resources, wasting budget, or accidentally deleting a live website.

And just like you wouldn't keep your entire client list on a local Excel file on your laptop, we don't store State files locally. We store them securely in a centralized cloud backend to ensure team collaboration and data security.
